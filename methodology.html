Scoring Methodology

This page describes the criteria, weighting, and discretization process used to score each cell of the 3D x 9T Regulatory Governance Matrix.

Framework Overview

The matrix evaluates the regulatory readiness of Brazil's federal framework for Urban Air Mobility (UAM) operations, specifically electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft integration into urban environments.

The framework intersects 3 governance dimensions with 9 thematic areas, yielding 27 assessment cells. Each cell is scored using three complementary criteria (C1, C2, C3) that capture different facets of regulatory maturity.

Scoring Criteria (C1, C2, C3)

Each cell is assessed on three independent criteria, each scored on a continuous 0–10 scale:

C1 — UAM Specificity (C1_especificidade_uam)

Measures the degree to which existing regulatory instruments explicitly address UAM/eVTOL operations, as opposed to covering the theme only through generic aviation or urban planning provisions.

RangeMeaning
0–3No UAM-specific provisions; coverage only by analogy or general legislation
4–6Some provisions applicable to UAM through adaptation of existing rules
7–10Explicit UAM/eVTOL references in normative instruments

Input: Computed from the corpus analysis pipeline based on document keyword matching and semantic relevance scoring. The metric is derived from the ratio of UAM-direct documents (n_uam_direto) to total relevant documents (n_docs_relevantes), weighted by document relevance scores.

C2 — Operationality (C2_operacionalidade)

Expert-assessed criterion reflecting whether the regulatory provisions identified can be operationally applied to UAM scenarios in their current form, without requiring new rulemaking.

RangeMeaning
0–3Instruments exist but are inoperative or inapplicable to UAM without major reform
4–6Instruments are partially operative; adaptations or interpretive guidance needed
7–10Instruments are directly operative for UAM with minimal or no adaptation

Input: Researcher qualitative judgment. Each C2 score is accompanied by a written justification note (c2_nota) that documents the reasoning and evidence considered. These notes are visible in the cell detail view of the interactive matrix.

C3 — Institutional Competence (C3_competencia_inst)

Measures the clarity and robustness of the institutional mandate held by relevant regulatory bodies for the specific theme.

RangeMeaning
0–3No body has a clear mandate for this theme in the UAM context
4–6One or more bodies have related mandates, but coordination gaps or overlaps exist
7–10Clear institutional mandate with defined competencies for the theme

Input: Derived from institutional mapping of regulatory bodies (orgaos_presentes) identified in the corpus, cross-referenced with their legal mandates.

Weighting and Aggregation

The three criteria are combined into a single raw score using a weighted average:

score_raw = w1 · C1 + w2 · C2 + w3 · C3

where w1 = 0.35, w2 = 0.35, w3 = 0.30. These weights reflect the roughly equal importance of specificity and operationality, with a slightly lower weight for institutional competence (which tends to be higher across the board due to Brazil's mature institutional framework).

The resulting score_raw_010 is on a continuous 0–10 scale.

Discretization: The 0-1-2 Scale

For the heatmap visualization and policy interpretation, the continuous raw score is discretized into a three-level ordinal scale:

Discrete ScoreRaw RangeInterpretationColor
0 0.00 – 3.33 Absent / Critical Gap — No meaningful regulatory coverage for UAM in this cell. New instruments or institutional mandates are required. Red
1 3.34 – 6.66 Partial / Analogous Coverage — Some regulatory provisions exist but are generic, require adaptation, or lack UAM specificity. Incremental reform or guidance may suffice. Amber
2 6.67 – 10.00 Adequate / UAM-Specific — Regulatory provisions explicitly address UAM/eVTOL with operational clarity and institutional backing. Green

Normative Corpus

The assessment is based on a curated corpus of 56 federal binding normative instruments from Brazilian regulatory bodies including:

  • ANAC — Agência Nacional de Aviação Civil (Civil Aviation Authority)
  • DECEA — Departamento de Controle do Espaço Aéreo (Airspace Control Department)
  • ANATEL — Agência Nacional de Telecomunicações (Telecommunications Authority)
  • ANEEL — Agência Nacional de Energia Elétrica (Electricity Authority)
  • CONAMA — Conselho Nacional do Meio Ambiente (National Environment Council)
  • IBAMA — Instituto Brasileiro do Meio Ambiente (Environmental Institute)
  • Congresso Nacional — Federal legislation (Laws, Decrees)

Inclusion criteria: only federally binding instruments (laws, decrees, resolutions, regulations, technical instructions) are considered. Non-binding documents (technical standards, municipal plans, NBR/ABNT norms) are excluded per ADR-009 of the thesis methodology.

Limitations

  • Federal scope only: The matrix assesses federal-level instruments. State and municipal regulations, which are critical for territorial integration (D3), are not in scope.
  • Temporal snapshot: Scores reflect the normative corpus as collected during the research period (2024–2026). UAM regulation is evolving rapidly.
  • C2 subjectivity: The operationality criterion (C2) is based on researcher judgment. Sensitivity analysis and planned Delphi validation mitigate this limitation.
  • Binary presence: C1 captures specificity through keyword and semantic matching; nuances of regulatory quality beyond presence/absence are addressed through C2.
